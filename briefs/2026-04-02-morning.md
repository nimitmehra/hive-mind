## I. What Happened

### Iran expanded its target geography to three Gulf states in 48 hours — striking a QatarEnergy tanker near Ras Laffan and Kuwait airport fuel tanks — while its missile arsenal approaches exhaustion and its accuracy improves.

The same day, an Iranian drone set fuel tanks ablaze at Kuwait International Airport, closed to commercial flights since early March (source: AGBI, Al Jazeera). Combined with the Al-Salmi VLCC attack at Dubai port on March 31, Iran struck three different Gulf states in 48 hours — the attack geography expanding systematically from the Strait itself to Dubai anchorage to Qatar waters to Kuwait airfield. Reports indicate Iran gave Qatar advance notice of the AQUA 1 strike, suggesting calculated escalation rather than uncontrolled violence; IRGC claimed responsibility, alleging Israeli ties to the vessel (source: Al Jazeera). Yet Iran's ballistic missile fire rate has collapsed 95% — from 480 on Day 1 to fewer than 25 per day — with roughly 2,410 of an estimated 2,500 pre-war arsenal expended and 75% of launch capacity destroyed per CENTCOM; the adaptation is quality over quantity, with RFE/RL reporting hit rates are increasing even as volume collapses, meaning raw depletion curves understate Iran's remaining disruptive capacity (source: Jerusalem Post, RFE/RL, CSIS, CENTCOM).
