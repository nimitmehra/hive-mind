### Iran expanded its attack geography to three Gulf states in 48 hours — striking a QatarEnergy tanker near Ras Laffan, Kuwait airport fuel tanks, and a VLCC at Dubai — while its missile arsenal nears exhaustion but accuracy improves with a pivot from volume to precision.

Three Iranian cruise missiles targeted Qatar on April 1 — two intercepted, one struck the Panama-flagged tanker AQUA 1, chartered to QatarEnergy, 17 nautical miles north of Ras Laffan; all 21 crew safe (source: QatarEnergy official statement, Al Jazeera, AGBI). The same day, an Iranian drone set fuel tanks ablaze at Kuwait International Airport, closed to commercial flights since early March (source: AGBI, Al Jazeera). Combined with the Al-Salmi VLCC attack at Dubai port on March 31, Iran struck three different Gulf states in 48 hours — the attack geography expanding systematically from the Strait itself to Dubai anchorage to Qatar waters to Kuwait airfield. Reports indicate Iran gave Qatar advance notice of the AQUA 1 strike, suggesting calculated escalation rather than uncontrolled violence; IRGC claimed responsibility, alleging Israeli ties to the vessel (source: Al Jazeera). Yet Iran's ballistic missile fire rate has collapsed 95% — from 480 on Day 1 to fewer than 25 per day — with roughly 2,410 of an estimated 2,500 pre-war arsenal expended and 75% of launch capacity destroyed per CENTCOM; the adaptation is quality over quantity, with RFE/RL reporting hit rates are increasing even as volume collapses (source: Jerusalem Post, RFE/RL, CSIS, CENTCOM). This means raw depletion curves understate Iran's remaining disruptive capacity — fewer missiles, but each one hits harder and targets civilian infrastructure (tankers, airports, data centers, desalination plants) rather than military installations.
