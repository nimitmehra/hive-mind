### The Iran-US ceasefire expires Wednesday April 22, not April 26 — the morning brief conflated it with the Lebanon-Israel ceasefire, compressing the forcing-function window by four days.
The operative two-week ceasefire began April 8 under Pakistani brokerage and expires Wednesday morning IST (CONFIRMED — CBS News, NBC News, NPR, Time, multi-chain). Trump's Sunday "Stone Age" Truth Social post explicitly referenced this Wednesday deadline, not the April 26 Lebanon-Israel ceasefire (Trump-brokered, 10-day). Sadjadpour's 72-hour post-*Touska* test window (Iran kinetic on a US asset by Tuesday 23:59 IST) and the Wednesday US-side ceasefire decision now sit back-to-back rather than six days apart. The substantive consequence is that Tuesday evening through Wednesday morning — roughly 36 hours from this update — contains the entire crisis's decision density. Wednesday's Nifty open captures Iran's overnight choice but precedes the US response; it becomes the single highest-information equity print of the week.

### Iran's Khatam al-Anbiya command published a "family aboard" rationale for *delaying* retaliation — a face-saving absorption framework, not a kinetic timetable.
Tasnim News Agency Monday carried the Iranian armed-forces joint command's statement that the constraint on retaliation is humanitarian: "due to the presence of some family members of the ship's personnel, [the armed forces] were constrained in order to preserve their lives… after ensuring the safety of the families and personnel, the powerful armed forces of the Islamic Republic of Iran will carry out the necessary action" (CONFIRMED via Tasnim, Reuters, BNO, Al Jazeera, NBC, CBC, Boston Globe — six independent chains; the framing is one-sided and unverifiable externally, but the statement itself is real). Sunday evening Zolfaghari said "soon"; Monday Khatam al-Anbiya published a conditional deferral that lets retaliation be postponed each cycle for as long as crew families remain in custody. This constructs an indefinite face-saving floor: non-retaliation attributable to humanitarian constraint rather than military weakness or diplomatic calculation, with the onus of escalation transferred to Washington (release the crew or accept indefinite deferral). CENTCOM has not commented on the crew timeline. The Sadjadpour 72-hour test was designed to be falsified by absorption; the absorption branch is now the higher-probability outcome, and the 2019 Aramco analogue — both sides demonstrating capability, neither crossing military-on-military — tracks.

### Pezeshkian and Sharif spoke for 45 minutes Monday, and Araghchi held his own call with Pakistan's Dar — Iran's civilian wing is conducting real diplomacy through the Pakistan channel despite IRNA's public Round 2 rejection.
Pakistani PM Shehbaz Sharif briefed President Pezeshkian on his Saudi-Qatar-Türkiye consultations and pledged Pakistan "fully committed to promoting peace and security" (CONFIRMED — PMO readout, Express Tribune, Outlook India, Iran International). Separately, FM Araghchi called Pakistan's FM Ishaq Dar, telling him the US "pursues malicious goals and lacks the necessary seriousness" — replicating IRNA's Sunday register while continuing to perform the FM role Kayhan and Tasnim have been attacking him for (CONFIRMED — Press TV, NCRI). The two calls together require a revision of this morning's "factional collapse" reading. What looked like civilian-wing isolation was choreographed soft-rejection: IRNA carries the public refusal for hardline audiences while Pezeshkian and Araghchi engage the mediator's principals privately. Kayhan posted no fresh Monday-evening Araghchi attack, suggesting tacit hardliner acceptance of the two-track. Pezeshkian has still not publicly defended Araghchi by name at 96+ hours — but the Sharif call substitutes implicit institutional backing. The practical implication is that Iran is engaging the Pakistan channel even while refusing the format; the morning's "diplomatic track dies" framing is too binary.

### Nifty closed +0.05% at 24,364.85 — India defied both the morning brief's -0.5 to -1.0% base and Sunday's -1.0 to -1.5% base on one bank's earnings print.
ICICI Bank's Q4 net profit printed ₹13,702 crore (+8.5% YoY, vs ₹12,652 crore expected; gross NPA 1.4%; provisions -89% YoY), delivering roughly 20-30 index points against a modelled crisis drag of -80 to -120 points (CONFIRMED — Business Standard, Republic World, Yahoo Finance; ICICI release). Sensex closed +0.03% at 78,520.30; Bank Nifty flat. Two structural reads follow. First, the Russian-waiver-renewal cushion (1.9 mbpd Russian flow preserved) is absorbing the Iranian-zero incremental much more cleanly than the morning priced — the CAD-stress tail is materially smaller than modelled. Second, USD/INR held at ₹93.05 on a Brent +5% day when mechanical transmission would have depreciated the rupee 0.3-0.6% — the RBI is in the tape, forward-selling or managing the NDF band without announcement. The deployment-signal implication cuts against the investor: when crisis premium is absorbed by bank earnings and visible-hand FX management, the "Nifty <21,500 on kinetic retaliation" entry condition becomes structurally harder to hit. Unless Wednesday's ceasefire collapses into kinetic strikes on Hormuz infrastructure, Indian equities will not offer a panic entry.

### TTF retraced from the morning's €46 intraday spike to ~€40.56 — the "cleanest weekend cascade" did not hold, and Lloyd's AWRP is moderating rather than stepping up.
European gas's +11% morning print faded back to within a half-euro of Friday's €41.59 close by midday GMT (CONFIRMED — OilPriceAPI, TradingEconomics), consistent with three plausible mechanisms: shoulder-season storage data arriving, the Khatam al-Anbiya absorption framing reaching European traders, and probability re-rating of Wednesday ceasefire renewal. Both data points partially vindicate Goldman's "paper pricing one-off" framework on the physical side: the hard-money markets are refusing to step-change on rhetoric alone. The morning brief's Monday CF Industries / Nutrien +5-8% bounce thesis weakens materially — the input-cost shock that would have forced it is not printing. Two structural items that do NOT soften: Tom's Hardware and J2 Sourcing confirm the helium "two-week clock" for semiconductor fabs with SK Hynix publicly forced to diversify and Seagate/WD already allocated for 2026, keeping the Intel/TSMC/Samsung Q2 allocation-notice trigger the most time-sensitive item in the graph; and Haaretz reports IDF commanders describing systematic demolition of south Lebanese towns under the buffer-zone policy — kinetic-quiet but materially eroding the April 26 ceasefire's renewal prospects regardless of rocket fire.
