## I. What Happened

### The ceasefire-extension deal is reportedly agreed in outline, unsigned in fact — and the two sides now describe the same text in incompatible terms. [UPDATED — the day's most consequential new intelligence]
US and Iranian negotiators have reached a tentative 60-day MOU — Hormuz reopened, Iran selling oil freely, the blockade lifted *in proportion to* restored shipping, an Iranian commitment not to pursue a weapon, with HEU disposal and enrichment as the first agenda items — but Trump ended a 29 May White House Situation Room meeting **without announcing a final determination**, and Iran has not confirmed acceptance (REPORTED — Axios, CNN, PBS; CONFIRMED non-signature — CNBC). Secretary Rubio framed the holdup as wording, "disagreements over a word, a sentence," projecting "a few days." The new and important development is a concrete, on-the-record contradiction in how each capital sells the draft to its own audience: Trump publicly insists the ~1,000 lbs of HEU "will be destroyed" and there will be "no sanctions, no money, no nothing," whereas Iran's semi-official **Fars pushed back that Trump "raised issues that contradict the provisions of the agreement's text,"** asserting the draft contains *no* destruction clause and that the "most important part" is the immediate release of **$12bn of Iran's frozen assets — with Iran refusing further negotiation until it is paid** (REPORTED — Fars, Iran's framing, contested). This is the one-side-framing pattern running in *both* directions at once. Treat the destruction clause (US framing) and the $12bn-first demand (Iran framing) as CLAIMED by their respective sides until a signed text exists. Underneath the talk, the blockade is emphatically *not* relaxing: on 30 May the US military **reportedly** disabled the Gambia-flagged bulk carrier *Lian Star* in the Gulf of Oman — the reported **sixth vessel interdicted** (single source, Iran International citing the US military — REPORTED) — and War Secretary Hegseth said the same day the "blockade is very much still in place" and Hormuz must stay "a toll-free strait" (CONFIRMED — Hegseth on record; Iran International on the interdiction). That sits directly against the MOU's "blockade lifted in proportion to restored shipping" term — a real inconsistency, not a paraphrase. Watch the next 48-72 hours for Trump's signature or a walk-away.

### Brent posted its biggest monthly loss since 2020 — the peace dividend, de-priced. [UPDATED — hard data; pairs with the deal]
Brent closed the final trading day of May at **$92.05, down 22% on the month** (CONFIRMED — CNBC, Trading Economics), and is roughly 20% off the 2026 peak (~$111 on 18 May) — the steepest monthly fall in five years, driven almost entirely by the market pricing a high probability that the Hormuz tail-risk premium gets removed under the MOU. This is tail-risk de-pricing on deal *anticipation*, not structural normalisation: Hormuz is still physically contested (the *Lian Star* interdiction, a fresh suspected mine off Oman — see continuing threads), and the sell-side desks CNBC canvassed see Brent holding **$90-100 "for at least the next couple of months"** until a lasting peace is clear — i.e. a real, not-vanished, premium persists even sub-$100. ### India fell again on a day of unambiguous crisis *relief* — the cleanest non-confirmation of the cycle. [UPDATED — new mechanical catalyst]
On 29 May the Nifty 50 closed **23,547.75, down 1.5%**, and the Sensex fell 1.4% to 74,775.7 — a third straight down session and the **worst May in six years** (−2.8% for the calendar month) — on a day when the crisis news was uniformly *good* for India: Brent −22% MoM, the rupee *firming* to ~₹95.0, the India 10Y easing to 7.00%, and the ceasefire MOU progressing. The textbook relief leg should have lifted the index; instead it fell. The proximate cause was mechanical, not geopolitical: **MSCI's Global Standard Index rebalance took effect at the close, triggering an estimated $800m-$1bn of foreign *passive* outflows** (REPORTED — Nuvama via wire), compounding F&O-expiry volatility and the structural FII rotation out of expensive India (~24x P/E vs a ~22x ten-year average) and into the Korea/Taiwan AI-memory trade. The macro hardened underneath: RBI forex reserves fell to **$681.4bn for the week to 22 May, a sharp −$7.5bn weekly drawdown** (CONFIRMED — RBI weekly supplement), now ~$37bn off peak, with cumulative war-period outflows above $22bn and FY26 net FPI near −$16.7bn. The intelligence here is decisive: India's weakness is now empirically *structural and mechanical, not crisis-linked* — even with every crisis variable moving its way, global capital reallocation to AI overwhelmed it. A Hormuz deal removes a headwind India was no longer trading on.

### Israel crossed Washington's Beirut red line — IDF inside Beirut and across the Litani, 142 killed in 72 hours — even as the first Pentagon Israel-Lebanon military talks opened the same day. [NEW/UPDATED — confirmed escalation]
On 29 May Netanyahu announced IDF forces had crossed the Litani and were "operating in Beirut and the Bekaa Valley," calling it a "tactical victory" — the first IDF action in Beirut in roughly three weeks (CONFIRMED — Netanyahu on record, Times of Israel). Israel issued sweeping evacuation orders for around seven southern towns ahead of strikes south of the Zahrani; the Lebanese health ministry reported **142 killed in the prior 72 hours, raising the cumulative toll to 3,355** (vs ~3,244 on 28 May), and IDF combat deaths reached 24 (CONFIRMED — Lebanese MoPH, IDF via timeline; the 28 May "22" baseline is reconciled upward to 24). Crucially, all of this ran *simultaneously* with the **first-ever direct Israel-Lebanon military talks at the Pentagon**, a six-member Lebanese military delegation and Israeli officials hosted by Under Secretary Elbridge Colby, which the Pentagon called "productive" and said would feed a State-led political track reconvening "next week" (CONFIRMED — The National, WaPo, Arab News). Hezbollah — which keeps fighting and is excluded from the underlying 45-day ceasefire — signs nothing. This is negotiate-while-bombing as a single coercive strategy, not two data points; it is also the deal's binding constraint, since Iran insists any agreement end the war "on all fronts including Lebanon."
