## What Changed Since Morning

### Trump explicitly ruled out extending the ceasefire — the deadline is now binary: deal by April 21 or the ceasefire lapses with no Plan B.

Trump told ABC News on April 15 that he is "not thinking about extending" the two-week ceasefire, saying "a deal is preferable" and "I think you're going to be watching an amazing two days ahead" (sources: SBS Australia citing ABC News, Anadolu Agency, Fox Business — CONFIRMED statement). This is a material hardening from the morning brief's "talk-about-talks" posture. Meanwhile, Reuters (4 sources) and AP (5 sources) report delegations are keeping Friday-Sunday (April 17-20) open for Islamabad Round 2, but no date is confirmed — White House: "nothing has been scheduled"; Iranian Embassy in Islamabad: "nothing is finalised as of now" (sources: Reuters, AP, TIME — REPORTED). VP Vance told Al-Monitor that Iranian negotiators "moved in our direction... but they didn't move far enough" and had to return to Tehran for approval — suggesting an authorization gap in the Iranian team. The ceasefire expires April 21-22 with 6-7 days remaining.

### The IEA's April report revealed the largest oil supply disruption on record — validating the physical oil market's pricing and contradicting the equity market's de-escalation narrative.

The IEA's April Oil Market Report, released April 14, found global oil supply fell 10.1 million bpd in March to 97M bpd — the largest disruption on record — and reversed its 2026 demand forecast from growth of 730,000 bpd to a contraction of 80,000 bpd, with Q2 expected to decline 1.5M bpd, the sharpest quarterly drop since COVID (sources: IEA Oil Market Report, Euronews, Al Jazeera — CONFIRMED). Separately, OPEC crude output collapsed 7.88M bpd to 20.79M bpd, the steepest monthly decline since the 1980s, while Russia's exports rose 270,000 bpd to 4.6M bpd — Moscow is the crisis beneficiary (sources: OPEC Monthly Report, IEA, Carnegie — CONFIRMED). The IEA's institutional weight validates what this morning's brief identified: the physical market at $132-144 Dated Brent reflects a genuine supply crisis that futures at $96 are not capturing. The Dated Brent figure has come down from last week's $144 peak to approximately $132, narrowing the physical-futures gap from ~$49 to ~$35 — still the widest sustained divergence of the crisis.

### Hezbollah fired approximately 30 rockets at northern Israel hours after the Washington talks concluded — a deliberate spoiler signal that defines the ceiling of the Lebanon diplomatic track.

The Israeli military detected ~30 Hezbollah rocket launches toward northern Israel on April 15, with one civilian injured. A Hezbollah political council member stated: "We are not bound by what they agree to" (sources: Xinhua, AP via Free Malaysia Today, Al Jazeera — CONFIRMED). This morning's brief covered Rubio's historic joint statement; Hezbollah's immediate kinetic response confirms the structural constraint identified there — Lebanese state diplomacy operates in one dimension while its most powerful armed group operates in another. Separately, Israel is expanding outposts in south Lebanon and bulldozing border towns like Naqoura for a buffer zone, against IDF officers' own warnings per Haaretz — scope creep that suggests the Lebanon operation has exceeded what military leadership believes is operationally sound.

### Three corrections and updates to this morning's brief:

**UK-France Hormuz conference is Friday April 17, not April 18** as this morning stated. Senior diplomat preparatory meetings begin April 16 (sources: Middle East Monitor, Bloomberg — CONFIRMED). Iran has now broken its silence on the initiative: the IRGC warned "any military vessels approaching the Strait will be dealt with harshly and decisively" via Fars News, and Iran's London ambassador said Tehran is "considering" strikes on British bases (sources: Fars News, Times Radio — CONFIRMED as statements). Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Oman, and Pakistan are NOT participating — only UAE and Bahrain from the Middle East.

**India's Iran sanctions waiver will NOT be renewed** per WION and Egypt Oil & Gas, citing US Treasury. This tightens the triple squeeze from this morning: the Russian waiver expired April 11, the Iran waiver expires April 19 with no extension coming, and the blockade constrains remaining routes. India's INR opened strong at Rs93.15-93.30 but settled weaker at Rs93.39 as importers aggressively bought dollars — the intraday reversal is more truthful than the headline strengthening number.

**Iran's official war damage figure is $270 billion** for the Feb 28 - April 7 period, lower than the $300B-$1T range cited this morning from Haass/Britannica — the government is positioning a conservative, credible reparations baseline ahead of resumed talks (sources: PressTV, Al Jazeera — CONFIRMED as Iran's claim).
