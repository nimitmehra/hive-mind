## What Changed Since Morning

### Pakistan hosted the first multilateral de-escalation initiative — foreign ministers from Saudi Arabia, Turkey, and Egypt joined Islamabad for talks aimed at establishing a formal mediation mechanism.

This is the most significant diplomatic development of the day. Pakistan's FM hosted Saudi Arabia's Prince Faisal bin Farhan, Turkey's Hakan Fidan, and Egypt's Badr Abdelatti in Islamabad on Saturday for what NPR described as an effort "to come up with a plan to de-escalate the Iran war" (sources: NPR, Jerusalem Post, The Nation Pakistan, Pravda Turkey independently confirmed). Turkish FM Fidan said the meeting would seek to "establish a mechanism aimed at de-escalation" — language suggesting an institutional framework, not just shuttle diplomacy. This quad represents Iran's four most capable interlocutors: Pakistan (confirmed message relay), Turkey (NATO member maintaining Iran ties), Egypt (Suez Canal leverage), Saudi Arabia (directly attacked, OPEC leader). Previously, mediation was purely bilateral — Pakistan relaying messages. A multilateral mechanism is qualitatively different. No joint statement yet; talks continue March 30.

### IDF launched "widespread" Saturday evening strikes across Tehran — hitting the same university the IRGC is threatening to avenge, as the Monday noon deadline approaches.

The IDF confirmed "a wide-scale wave of strikes targeting dozens of infrastructure belonging to the Iranian terror regime across Tehran" Saturday evening, hitting northeastern, western, central, and eastern parts of the city (sources: IDF statement, CNN live updates with geolocated video, Al Jazeera). CNN geolocated videos showing Iran University of Science and Technology's research center in ruins — the same institution whose destruction prompted the IRGC's retaliation threat. The IRGC escalated its language from this morning's "demand a condemnation" to declaring "all universities of the occupying regime and American universities in the West Asia region are legitimate targets" with a confirmed Monday March 30, noon Tehran time deadline (sources: Press TV, Republic World, WION). The IDF confirmed it had targeted Malek Ashtar University and Imam Hossein University, alleging both were IRGC weapons/space research facilities. The IRGC university deadline is the most immediate testable trigger for Monday — follow-through would represent an unprecedented targeting of civilian educational infrastructure across sovereign Gulf states.

### Iran fired 6 missile salvos at Israel during Saturday — the war's daily bombardment rhythm continues unbroken at one month.

Iran maintained its sustained missile campaign against Israel on Saturday, firing 6 salvos throughout the day (source: Times of Israel March 29 liveblog). This is neither an escalation nor a de-escalation — it's the steady-state rhythm of a one-month-old war. The Houthis did NOT launch a third attack on Saturday, and there were NO Red Sea shipping attacks. The double-chokepoint trigger remains at "watching."
