## I. What Happened

### Iran's 30-projectile barrage produced the conflict's first deaths — a drone killed an Indian national at Kuwait airport, 63 injured — but every strike on US forces failed, and the "Fifth Fleet HQ hit" was false. [UPDATED with NEW elements — the 1 June exchange escalated in scale and, for the first time, in consequence]
At dawn on Wednesday, Iran's IRGC fired **13 ballistic missiles and 17 drones** at targets across the Gulf — a step-change from the two intercepted missiles of 1 June (CONFIRMED — CENTCOM, Kuwait and Bahrain defence ministries, corroborated by Al-Monitor, RFE/RL, Arab News, Al Jazeera). CENTCOM said it "successfully defeated" the attacks on US forces, that several Iranian missiles "failed to reach their intended targets," and that **no American personnel or assets were harmed**; the US then struck an Iranian ground-control station on **Qeshm Island**, inside the Strait of Hormuz. The decisive new fact is where the lethal damage landed: an Iranian drone hit **Kuwait International Airport's terminal, killing one person and injuring 63** (seven critical), with damage to diplomatic missions; Bahrain's defence force said it intercepted three missiles and several drones aimed at "civilian sites" with no casualties. Iran's IRGC claimed it had struck the **US Fifth Fleet headquarters in Bahrain** and the Ali Al Salem airbase in Kuwait — **claims CENTCOM rejected as false** (treat as a one-side-framing artifact: the spectacular headline is the IRGC's; the verifiable reality per the targeted party is that the strikes failed and the damage landed on civilians). The read: Iran *aimed* at US military targets, *missed or was intercepted*, and the consequence fell on a Gulf mediator's civilians. The "no US casualties" calibration holds; the "no casualties at all" calibration of 1 June is gone — which is precisely the miscalculation channel that turns a managed exchange into an unmanaged one.

### The dead civilian was Indian — and Kuwait expelled two Iranian diplomats, a mediator host turning openly on Tehran. [NEW — the single most India-relevant development of the war, plus a rupture in the mediation architecture]
India's Ministry of External Affairs confirmed an **Indian national was killed** and several others were among the 63 injured, condemned the attack, urged that "civilian population and civilian infrastructure must not be targeted," and — the operative line — said **"Indian missions and posts across the region are on alert"** (CONFIRMED — MEA via Business Standard, The Tribune, IANS; Al Jazeera corroborating). This is the first time the ~8-million-strong Gulf diaspora has taken a direct casualty. It is *not yet* an evacuation posture — no Air India surge, no travel-advisory upgrade — but "missions region-wide on alert" is a measurable step up India's escalation ladder. Separately, **Kuwait declared two Iranian embassy staff *persona non grata*** with 24 hours to leave, condemning "the criminal Iranian aggression" against civilian infrastructure (CONFIRMED — Gulf News, Arab News). A Gulf state that hosts US forces but had stayed low-profile is now in open diplomatic rupture with Tehran — compounding the strategic cost of the barrage (Iran killed a civilian and alienated a neighbour to make a leverage point) and degrading the regional mediation environment at the exact moment the Iran-US channel is suspended. Watch whether Bahrain, Qatar and the UAE follow Kuwait or hold the mediator-neutral line; Qatar, tellingly, has not condemned.

### On the day of the conflict's first deaths, the entire metals complex sold off — the market is pricing this as an inflation event, not a risk-off event. [NEW — the cleanest market regime read of the cycle; sharpens yesterday's "gold won't bid"]
This is the morning's most important market signal. Oil extended its re-escalation leg — **Brent +2.2% to ~$98** (intraday tagging ~$101), WTI +2.6%, Heating Oil +4.1% on distillate tightness — but the metals reaction inverted everything a "crisis day" textbook would predict: **Platinum −3.5%, Palladium −3.0%, Silver −2.4%, Copper −2.3%, and gold managing only −0.5%** (CONFIRMED — market data). The channel: oil re-escalation feeds already-hot US inflation, so the market repriced the Fed path hawkishly — pricing rate cuts *further out* (CME FedWatch: ~65% odds of a hold at the June meeting; the median path now puts cuts toward Q4 2026/Q1 2027, not an outright hike) — lifting the US 10Y to **4.49%** and the dollar (DXY) +0.3%, while the 2Y held flat at **3.62%** (the front end is *not* pricing a hike). Higher real yields plus a stronger dollar compete directly with non-yielding metals. Copper carries an extra demand-destruction signal. This is not a safe-haven failure; it is the market reading the crisis as an *inflation* shock (bad for metals versus real yields) rather than a *financial-system* shock (which would bid them). The corroborating non-mover: **VIX 16.04 (+1.7%)** — barely a flicker on a day with the cycle's first deaths, because US equities are AI-led and decoupled (S&P −0.5%, Nasdaq −0.8% is an orderly pullback) and the tape does not price third-country civilian casualties. The asymmetry is the trade: VIX at 16 is priced for the calibration to hold. A *non-calibrated* act — US or allied-military deaths, or an operational Hormuz closure — is not in this price, and on that event gold and silver gap *up* and this entire chain reverses.

The asymmetry is now starker than ever: re-escalation hands India the oil-import *headwind* (and a tariff headwind) with no offsetting *relief*, because the binding driver is FII flight into the North-Asia AI trade, not the oil bill.
