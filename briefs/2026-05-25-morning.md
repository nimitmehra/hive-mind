### Trump bolted a "mandatory" Abraham Accords condition onto the Iran deal — six nations told to sign, met with "silence on the line." [NEW — largest scope-expansion of the cycle]
In Monday Truth Social posts Trump wrote that negotiations "are proceeding nicely!" and then declared it "should be mandatory" that Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Pakistan, Egypt, Jordan and Turkey "simultaneously, sign onto the Abraham Accords," telling Saudi and Qatar to "go first" and inviting Iran itself to join the "World Coalition" (CONFIRMED — NPR, Washington Post, CNBC, Times of Israel May 25). This is the operative emergence of the Lindsey Graham "expansionist" vector flagged in earlier briefs — now Trump policy, and the single largest scope-expansion of the cycle. The other-side reaction was the tell: per Axios's Barak Ravid, when Trump floated it on Saturday's leaders' call "there was silence on the line, and Trump joked and asked if they are still there" (REPORTED — Axios single-source on the reaction). UAE's MbZ, already a signatory, supports it; Saudi conditions normalisation on "an irreversible pathway to Palestinian statehood"; Pakistan, which declined the Accords under Imran Khan, is in a "difficult position." Bolting Arab-Israeli normalisation onto a Hormuz/nuclear de-escalation deal hands every reluctant capital a fresh reason to slow-walk — most likely an opening-bid overreach, but one that raises the activation energy for any signature.

### Iran's economic principals flew to Doha to negotiate frozen assets — and its foreign ministry said no deal is imminent. [NEW — substantive venue + a money track]
A senior Iranian delegation led by Parliament Speaker Ghalibaf, with FM Araghchi and — critically — Central Bank governor Abdolnaser Hemmati, arrived in Doha on Monday to work "issues related to the Strait of Hormuz and highly enriched uranium" and the unfreezing of Iranian assets, meeting Qatar's prime minister (CONFIRMED — Euronews, Daily Sabah, AP, Press TV, CNN). Fars framed the trade as "Washington has agreed to release part of Tehran's frozen overseas assets" in exchange for lifting Hormuz restrictions; the central-bank governor's presence confirms the frozen-asset mechanics are now a live, technical negotiating workstream, not rhetoric — Qatar shuttling, with the US not physically at the table. Yet Iran's FM spokesman Baghaei cooled expectations hard: "we have reached conclusions on many issues… but no one can claim… an imminent agreement is about to be signed," nuclear "isn't part of discussions at this stage," and — the sharpest new Iranian line — of Washington, "within just a few hours you can encounter completely different, and in many cases contradictory, positions" (CONFIRMED — NBC, CNBC, AP). Rubio matched the cooling, calling the talks "still a work in progress." Iran is publicly weaponising Trump's own oscillation as a negotiating point and defending its 30-60-day procedural floor.
