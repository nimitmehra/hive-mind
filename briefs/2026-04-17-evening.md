## What Changed Since Morning

### Brent recovered from the morning lows; the net day is -3.3%, not -6.5% — and the Brent-WTI spread blew out to $8.39, the widest since the blockade began.

This morning's brief led with Brent at $92.92 (-6.5%) and framed the futures-physical gap at $39. By Friday settlement Brent had bounced to $96.16 — a net -3.3% on the day — as the US electronic session opened and the Pakistani-flagged tanker Shalamar (approximately 440,000 barrels bound for Karachi) confirmed a Hormuz transit that reinforced the "port blockade, not strait closure" framing established by JCS Chairman Caine on April 16 (sources: yfinance BZ=F, CNN liveblog — confirmed; Shalamar via CNN single source — REPORTED). WTI diverged the other way, settling at $87.77 (-7.3%) and widening the Brent-WTI spread to $8.39, the widest since the blockade began. The mechanism matters: Brent is held up by Dated Brent physical at ~$132 (Thursday print, not re-quoted Friday) and Hormuz-intact port blockade; WTI is freer to price deal-optimism without a Gulf-delivery hedge. The physical-futures gap narrows to ~$36 — only because futures bounced, not because physical eased — and remains the widest sustained divergence of the crisis. CF Industries' +3.2% Thursday counter-directional move still stands as the cleanest equity-market evidence that the supply-disruption mechanism operates independently of paper oil. Heating oil at -8.7% was the largest single-asset move of the day, the distillate-supply-crisis trade fully unwinding.

### The Lebanon ceasefire's first 24 hours were asymmetric — Hezbollah complied cleanly; Israel's "self-defense" violations began within hours — and Iran's FM re-linked the pause to the broader US-Iran deal that Washington had publicly de-linked.

The morning brief framed the 10-day Israel-Lebanon ceasefire as "the first structured military pause since the war began." The evening story is that the pause is already degraded. Lebanon's army reported Israeli violations: artillery and machine-gun fire on the villages of Al-Khiam and Debbin, and drone attacks in the Western Bekaa — within hours of the midnight Beirut start (sources: Al Jazeera, Asia Plus, Lebanese Army statement — confirmed). Israel's procedural response — "the IDF reserves the right to take all necessary measures in self-defense" (CNN) — effectively pre-authorised continued strikes under Netanyahu's 10-kilometre security zone. Hezbollah confirmed that it "launched its last attack at 11:50pm local time, just 10 minutes before the ceasefire took effect" — clean compliance on its side (sources: Hezbollah statement via Al Jazeera — confirmed). Iran's Foreign Ministry spokesperson Baghaei then tightened the linkage Washington had been working to sever: the Lebanon ceasefire, he said, is "part of a broader agreement with the US to pause the regional war, mediated by Pakistan," and Israel must "fully withdraw forces from southern Lebanon" — directly contesting the 10km zone (sources: Baghaei via Press TV, Al Jazeera, CNN — confirmed). Baghaei also attacked US sanctions as "economic terrorism… crimes against humanity and… genocide" — the two-track "negotiate AND resist" posture tightening, not loosening. Washington has not publicly responded.

### Trump's Round 2 rhetoric tightened to "this weekend"; Pakistan's "no date set" is unchanged; no US delegation has travelled.

President Trump told CNN on April 17 that a deal is "very close" and suggested talks could resume in Islamabad "as early as this weekend" — a material tightening from the Las Vegas "next week or so" framing of April 16 (source: CNN April 17 liveblog — confirmed as statement). But Pakistan's Foreign Ministry position of "no date had been set" (April 16, via Al Jazeera) has not been superseded by any Pakistani announcement today, and no Witkoff, Vance, or Kushner travel to Islamabad has been reported. Parliament Speaker Ghalibaf, acknowledging Hezbollah's role in the ceasefire chain, warned of "approaching developments with caution" — a measurable down-shift from his pre-Round 1 "inches from an MoU" rhetoric (source: Ghalibaf via Al Jazeera/CNN — confirmed). The nuclear-enrichment impasse that collapsed Round 1 is unchanged: US wants a 20-year enrichment pause, Iran wants five; Witkoff's "zero enrichment" red line (per Responsible Statecraft) is incompatible with Iran's minimum. The single most important non-event of the evening is the absence of any US delegation movement toward Pakistan despite the "this weekend" framing. If the weekend ends without a delegation in Islamabad, the morning's deal-optimism pricing requires a material downgrade Monday.

### The Macron-Starmer Hormuz summit is smaller than the morning brief suggested — 30-40 nations virtual, not 70-80 in-person.

A CNN/ABC fact-check correction: the Paris summit described in the morning brief as drawing 70-80 nations with Chancellor Merz and PM Meloni in person was in fact a 30-40-nation virtual meeting (sources: CNN "40-nation virtual summit"; ABC/ClickOnDetroit "about 30 countries" — confirmed). Macron reiterated the mission would be "strictly defensive… deployed when security conditions allow" — a post-war framework, not a near-term commercial escort. The US is excluded as a warring party; from the Middle East, only UAE and Bahrain attended — Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Oman, and Pakistan were absent, confirming the Gulf's preference for the Pakistan-led diplomatic track over a Europe-led security track. Separately, UAE VP Sheikh Mansour bin Zayed phoned Parliament Speaker Ghalibaf — the second UAE-Iran parliamentary-channel call in 72 hours, parallel to Saudi Arabia's "daily contact" posture (source: Tasnim via Al Jazeera — confirmed).

### Nifty made a post-ceasefire high of 24,353; RBI forex reserves rebounded $9.063B on valuation, not intervention reversal; Sunday's waiver expiry remains the uncovered fragility.

Nifty 50 closed at 24,353.55 (+0.65%) — the highest close since the April 8 ceasefire — with Sensex at 78,493.54 recovering all but approximately 1.7% of its crisis-induced losses and the rupee strengthening to Rs92.84 on the Brent crash (source: yfinance — confirmed). The RBI's weekly forex reserve print rose $9.063B to $697.121B for the week ending April 3 — but the trajectory is the signal: a peak of $728.494B on February 27, a trough of $688B in early April, now a valuation-effect rebound to $697B, leaving the reserves ~$31B below pre-crisis. Tomorrow's release, covering the week ending April 10, will capture the post-blockade intervention week (source: RBI weekly release via bizzbuzz.news — confirmed). The uncomfortable arithmetic: the Indian equity complex is pricing rhetoric-driven relief going into a 72-hour weekend that spans Sunday's April 19 Iran oil sanctions waiver expiry — the one binary catalyst that is certain to arrive. The Russian waiver already expired April 11. India has built a 30-60M-barrel Russian buffer and secured approximately 4M Iranian, but Monday's open must absorb both sanctioned doors slamming shut while Hormuz remains port-blockaded.
