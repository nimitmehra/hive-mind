## What Changed Since Morning

### Saudi Arabia is on the de-escalation track — MBS hosted Pakistan PM Shehbaz in Jeddah and the Saudi FM is talking to Tehran "on a daily basis." This morning's "MBS hanging back" framing was wrong.

This morning's brief read Saudi Arabia's silence as deliberate leverage-preservation while the UAE moved first. By evening that framing has collapsed. Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman received Pakistan PM Shehbaz Sharif in Jeddah on April 16 as the opening leg of Sharif's four-country tour (Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Turkiye, 15-18 April) explicitly framed as "building momentum ahead of a possible second round of US-Iran negotiations in Pakistan"; the readout from MBS's office said the two leaders "stressed the importance of sustained diplomatic engagement to restore stability" (sources: Arab News, Pakistan Today, Al Jazeera, TRT World, Tribune Pakistan, CONFIRMED). Saudi Arabia separately committed $3 billion to Pakistan's forex reserves the same day. Arab News, citing a Saudi official, then stated explicitly that "Saudi officials are talking to Iran on a daily basis," and Al Arabiya confirmed Saudi FM Faisal bin Farhan held a phone call with Iran's Araghchi on April 13 reviewing "developments following the Iran-US talks in Islamabad" (CONFIRMED, both sides). Read together with the morning's UAE-Iran call, the Gulf institutional position is now unanimously pro-Round 2 — Saudi via a quieter FM-FM channel, UAE publicly, Qatar via Al Jazeera framing, Pakistan running the shuttle. The thesis to track: this multi-track Gulf-Iran warming may be substantial enough to substitute for a slow US-Iran deal in pricing terms, but not enough to repair Hormuz insurance or the physical-futures Brent gap (war-risk premium unchanged at 0.8-1.0% fourth day running).

### The Brent-WTI spread blew out to $7 on the same session — a two-tier oil world is now visible in the futures tape itself.

WTI front-month fell 2.9% to $88.67 after the EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report showed a +6.10 million-barrel US crude inventory build against a -1.3M expectation (the fifth straight weekly build, total stocks 464.7M); Brent rose 0.9% to $95.79 on the Iran waiver expiring April 19, CENTCOM's Day-5 "fully implemented" blockade claim, and OPEC+ holding cuts (sources: EIA, Trading Economics, investingLive, Military Times, CONFIRMED data and CLAIMED on CENTCOM comprehensiveness). Two oil benchmarks moved in opposite directions on the day Saudi joined the de-escalation track — the spread should have compressed and instead doubled. The signal: WTI is trading the US-domestic story (refining demand soft, structural insulation from Hormuz); Brent is trading the global supply-risk story (blockade Day 5, Iran waiver T-3, no signed framework). India imports Brent-linked crude — the morning's "oil softening into Nifty" framing now has a refined caveat: WTI is softening, Brent is not, and the price India actually pays is the one staying bid. The EIA's own April STEO forecast called for the spread to peak near $15 in April; today's $7 leaves further room before that ceiling.

### Indian markets faded the morning rally into the close — Nifty -0.1% (24,196.75), Sensex -0.2% (77,989) — FII selling did not take the day off for ceasefire optimism.

This morning's brief carried the 12:00 IST snapshot — Nifty +0.3% to 24,300, Sensex +0.7% to 78,682 — as the table value. The 15:30 IST close inverted that: Nifty -0.1% at 24,196.75, Sensex -0.2% at 77,988.68, Bank Nifty -0.4% at 56,086.40 (sources: Kotak Neo, Dhan, TradingView, 5paisa, BSE/NSE feeds, CONFIRMED). The reversal was FII-led; DIIs remained net buyers. The Sensex top losers were oil-sensitive heavyweights led by Reliance, whose four Iranian-crude vessels will not clear before the April 19 waiver expires. The disconnect from the rest of Asia is now sharp — Nikkei +2.4%, KOSPI +2.2%, TAIEX +1.1% on the TSMC Q1 blowout, while Nifty alone closed red. Asia is trading the AI cycle; India is trading its own triple squeeze. Partial offset on the macro tail: RBI forex reserves jumped $9.063B to $697.121B in the week ended April 3 (the largest single weekly addition of the crisis), buying perhaps 4-6 weeks of additional INR-defense runway — material but not stock-flow-decisive against an FY26 FII outflow above Rs 3.3 trillion.

### TSMC Q1 crushed estimates and AI demand was called "extremely robust" — the AI capex cycle is now confirmed independent of the Iran deal.

Taiwan Semiconductor reported Q1 2026 revenue of $35.9 billion (above the high end of its own guide range of $34.6-35.8B), EPS of NT$22.08 versus Street $20.88, gross margin 66.2% versus consensus 64.5%; Q2 guide was $39-40.2B versus Street $38.1B; FY revenue guidance held at >30% USD growth and capex was lifted to the high end of its $52-56B range (sources: Sherwood News, TheStreet, Investing.com transcript, TipRanks, TSMC IR, CONFIRMED). Management's "extremely robust" AI demand line is the single most important non-crisis bull-data point of the quarter — it confirms with a hard print what the S&P/NASDAQ ATH had been pricing on rhetoric. The implication for the Iran-thesis investor: the AI capex leg of the equity rally now has independent footing, so a ceasefire collapse would not necessarily unwind the full S&P/NASDAQ run — only the ~25-30% of it that can be attributed to Iran-deal pricing rather than to TSMC-confirmed AI capex. The helium signal still cuts the other way (Henan price 170 yuan/m³, +120% since March) but TSMC's multi-year forward contracts insulate it; Korean memory players are the more exposed cohort, with SK Hynix Q1 later this month the next test.

### Mossad chief Barnea publicly named regime change as the war aim; IDF Chief Zamir framed Israel as in a "multi-theater campaign." Israeli operational autonomy on Lebanon is now an explicit feature of the ceasefire window, not a bug.

Mossad director David Barnea stated publicly that the Iran campaign "will only be complete when this extremist regime is replaced" — the most explicit regime-change articulation by a serving Israeli intelligence chief since the war began (source: Algemeiner, CONFIRMED primary statement). IDF Chief of Staff Lt. Gen. Eyal Zamir said Israel was "facing a multi-theater campaign unprecedented in the history of our people," adding the military "remained on high alert and was continuing to approve operational plans for both Lebanon and Iran" (source: Times of Israel, CONFIRMED). On the ground the same day, Israeli strikes hit southern Lebanon overnight and into Wednesday, killing at least 20 per Lebanon's National News Agency (sources: Washington Times, Al Jazeera, PBS, CONFIRMED). A senior US official continued telling Haaretz that Lebanon is "not part of the peace negotiations with Iran" — the on-record de-linking holds. Netanyahu publicly framed Israel and the US as sharing "identical goals" (Outlook India), but operationally Israel keeps striking Lebanon while the US de-links Lebanon from the Iran track — the "identical" line is aspirational. Implication: any US-Iran framework that preserves regime continuity will be tested from inside the ceasefire window by Israeli action on the Lebanon track. If Round 2 lands, it will land on a structurally fragile floor.
