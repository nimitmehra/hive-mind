### Day 1 — February 28, 2026: The Attack

**What happened:**

- **Targets:** Iranian leadership compound (Leadership House in Tehran), nuclear infrastructure at Natanz, Fordow, and Isfahan, missile facilities, naval forces. (Sources: Al Jazeera, Xinhua, Britannica, FDD analysis)

**Supreme Leader Khamenei killed:**
- Initial Iranian denial: Iran's MFA spokesman Esmail Baghaei said Khamenei was "safe and sound." Reuters reported transfer to a "secure location." (Sources: Wikipedia/Assassination article, Times of Israel)
- Before midnight Iran time, an unnamed Israeli official confirmed Khamenei had been killed and body recovered. Netanyahu said "growing signs" pointed to death. Trump called it "justice for the people of Iran." (Sources: Times of Israel liveblog, NPR, CNBC)
- **Confirmed March 1** by Iranian state media. Also killed: Chief of Staff Abdolrahim Mousavi and former president Mahmoud Ahmadinejad. Mojtaba Khamenei's mother, wife, and one sister were also killed in the strike. (Sources: Al Jazeera, NPR, Jerusalem Post, multiple outlets)

**Other killed officials:** Several senior IRGC officials (Sources: Xinhua, Al Jazeera, Wikipedia)

**Iran's immediate response (same day):**
- ~08:30 GMT: IRGC announced large-scale missile and drone attacks.
- Iranian missiles struck US military bases across the region. Explosions reported in Bahrain, UAE, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, and Jordan. (Sources: Al Jazeera, Xinhua timeline)

**Casualties (Day 1):**
- Iranian Red Crescent: strikes across 24 provinces killed 201 people and injured 747. (Source: Xinhua timeline, Al Jazeera live tracker)

**Israel and US military also confirmed attacks:**
- ~06:30 GMT: Israel's Defense Minister Israel Katz confirmed "preemptive" strike, declared nationwide state of emergency. (Source: Xinhua, Times of Israel)
- ~07:00 GMT: Israel and Iran both closed airspace. (Source: Xinhua)
- ~07:30 GMT: Trump said US launched "large-scale military campaign to destroy Iran's missile industry, naval forces, and nuclear capabilities." (Source: Xinhua, CNBC, NPR)

## PART 3: COUNTRY-BY-COUNTRY IMPACT

### IRAN
- **Leadership:** Supreme Leader Khamenei killed Feb 28. Son Mojtaba Khamenei appointed Mar 8. No video/audio of new leader released as of Mar 12.
- **Military damage:** Nuclear sites (Natanz, Fordow, Isfahan) struck multiple times. Kharg Island (90% of oil exports) military sites destroyed. South Pars gas field (70% of gas production) damaged (~12% output loss). IRIB state broadcaster HQ destroyed.
- **Casualties:** 5,300+ killed in 18 days (Hengaw); 1,400+ civilians (HRANA). Government figures: 1,255 killed (as of Mar 9).
- **Retaliation:** 500+ ballistic missiles, 2,000 drones fired. Struck Israel (Beit Shemesh, Jerusalem, Dimona), UAE (314 ballistic, 1,672 drones, 15 cruise), Bahrain, Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia, Oman, Iraq (US bases).
- **Missile depletion:** Launch rates declining from Day 1, suggesting rationing/depletion.
- **Diplomatic position:** FM Araghchi: "The United States started it and is responsible." Rejects ceasefire talks. Pezeshkian demands reparations, recognition, guarantees.

### ISRAEL
- **Operations:** Launched strikes on Iran, expanded ground incursion into Lebanon (against Hezbollah).
- **Casualties:** At least 18 killed by Iranian missiles. 180+ wounded in Dimona area strikes (Mar 19). Beit Shemesh: 9 killed, 65 injured (synagogue hit).
- **Domestic:** Nationwide state of emergency. Air raid sirens. Netanyahu framing as "preemptive" defense.
- **Lebanon front:** Ground operations in southern Lebanon. Strikes on Beirut. ~1,000 Lebanese killed, 1M+ displaced.

### UNITED STATES
- **Military:** Operation Epic Fury. 50,000 troops deployed around Middle East. Additional 2,500 Marines + 3 warships sent. Cost: ~$2B/day, $11.3B in first 6 days. Pentagon requesting $200B supplemental.
- **Political:** Trump sending mixed signals — "winding down" rhetoric vs. threatening power plants and deploying more troops. Lifted some Iran oil sanctions (reversal). GOP resistance to $200B funding.
- **Strategy questions:** Axios reported Trump considering Kharg Island occupation. CNN analysis: "no clear end in sight."

### INDIA
- **Oil impact:** Imports ~85-90% of crude; 50% of crude imports transit Hormuz. Additional $57.4B annual import cost at $108/bbl oil. India also imports ~60% of LPG from Gulf, ~91% of LPG from Gulf region.

- **Markets:** Nifty down 8.2%, Sensex down 8.3% since war began. FIIs sold ₹52,704 crore in March.
- **Citizens:** 3 lakh+ Indians returned from Gulf. 52,000 evacuated in first week. 6 Indian nationals killed (2 in Oman drone strike, 3 sailors, 1 in Riyadh).
- **Government response:** PM Modi chaired CCS meeting (Mar 22). Addressed Lok Sabha (Mar 23). Group of Ministers formed. Focus: oil, gas, fertilizer, power supply continuity.

- **Fertilizer:** Government assuring supply despite global price spikes. Qatar urea plant shutdown directly affects India.
- **RBI:** Held rates, buying bonds (₹1T), heavy forex intervention. Strategy: "calibrated, smooth depreciation."

## PART 4: EMERGING CONNECTIONS (CASCADE PATHS)

These are the verified connections that emerged from the data — the basis for knowledge graph edges:

### 1. Iran Attack → Khamenei Death → Mojtaba Succession → Unpredictable Leadership
The killing of Khamenei was the single most consequential act. His son, untested as leader, has not appeared on video. The regime's decision-making under a new, unknown leader creates unpredictability.

### 2. Hormuz Closure → Oil Shock → India CAD → Rupee Collapse → RBI Dilemma
This is the most direct cascade to India. Hormuz closure → 50% of India's crude imports at risk → oil at $100-120 → additional $57B import bill → rupee at record lows → RBI forced to choose between defending currency (selling reserves) and supporting growth (rate cuts). Currently choosing currency defense at cost of reserves.

### 3. South Pars Attack → Iran Retaliates on Qatar → LNG Crisis → European Gas Spike → Fertilizer Crisis
Israel struck South Pars → Iran struck Ras Laffan in retaliation → 17% of Qatar LNG capacity destroyed (5yr repair) → European TTF gas doubled → Qatar urea plant shutdown → urea prices +40% → India fertilizer supply at risk (India imports from Gulf).

### 4. Ras Laffan Damage → Helium Shortage → Semiconductor Supply Risk → AI Infrastructure → Tech Valuations
Qatar produces 30% of world's helium at Ras Laffan. Ras Laffan offline → helium supply cut → semiconductor wafer cooling requires helium (no substitute) → 2-3 month minimum disruption → HP/Dell/Lenovo warned of 15-20% price hikes → AI chip supply uncertainty → tech valuation risk.

### 5. Saudi Yanbu Bypass → But Yanbu Also Attacked → No Safe Export Route
Saudi activated East-West pipeline to Yanbu (4.2M bpd) to bypass Hormuz. But Iran attacked Yanbu too (drone, Mar 18). This means even the bypass route is not secure. If both Hormuz AND Yanbu are under threat, Gulf oil exports have no reliable outlet.

### 6. Iraq Force Majeure → Additional Supply Shock → Oil Spike → Stagflation Risk
Iraq cut production from 3.3M to 900K bpd. Iraq depends on Hormuz for exports. This is an additional 2.4M bpd off the market ON TOP of Iranian production offline.

### 7. Oil → Inflation → Fed Can't Cut → Bond Yields Rise → Equity Pressure
Oil spike → US inflation pressure → Fed held rates, market pricing HIKE → 10Y at 4.37% → higher discount rates → equity valuations compressed. Gold falling (dollar strength). The traditional "war = buy gold" playbook is broken.

### 8. Defense/Energy Rotation ← War → Tech Selloff
Capital rotating from tech/growth into defense and energy stocks. Lockheed +15%, Exxon +5%+, Cheniere Energy gaining. NASDAQ falling. This is a structural sector rotation, not just a dip.

### 9. Houthi Restraint → Strategic Wildcard
Houthis NOT attacking (yet) is a major surprise. If they enter the war, the Red Sea (already disrupted from 2023-2024 crisis) could close again, creating a SECOND shipping chokepoint alongside Hormuz. This would compound the Cape of Good Hope rerouting already happening.

### 10. Lebanon War → Israel Two-Front Problem
Israel simultaneously fighting Iran (air) and Hezbollah (ground in Lebanon). 1M Lebanese displaced. This divides Israeli resources and creates humanitarian crisis that could draw in more actors.
